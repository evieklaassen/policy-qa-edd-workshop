# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Document Owner:** People Operations  
**Policy ID:** HR-PTO-001

---

## 1. Overview

Acme Corp provides paid time off (PTO) to all full-time employees. PTO is intended to cover vacation, personal days, and short-term illness. Part-time employees working 20+ hours/week receive prorated PTO.

---

## 2. PTO Accrual by Region

### United States (All States except California)
| Tenure | Annual PTO Days | Accrual Rate |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| 0–1 year | 15 days | 1.25 days/month |
| 1–3 years | 18 days | 1.5 days/month |
| 3–5 years | 22 days | ~1.83 days/month |
| 5+ years | 25 days | ~2.08 days/month |

**Rollover:** Up to 5 days may be rolled over into the following calendar year. All unused PTO above 5 days is forfeited on December 31.

### California (USA)
California employees follow a **use-it-or-lose-it prohibition**. Acme Corp does not apply a hard cap or forfeiture policy in California. Instead:
- PTO accrues at the same rates as other US employees.
- **No year-end forfeiture.** Unused PTO rolls over indefinitely.
- A soft cap of 45 days is applied: accrual pauses when the cap is reached and resumes when balance drops below 45 days.
- Upon termination, all accrued and unused PTO is paid out at the employee's final base salary rate.

### Netherlands
- All employees receive the statutory minimum of **20 days** per year plus Acme Corp's supplemental **5 days**, totaling **25 days**.
- An additional **5 days** are granted after 3 years of tenure (30 days total).
- Dutch statutory days (20) expire 6 months after the end of the accrual year (July 1 of the following year). Supplemental days expire December 31 of the following year.
- Employees may carry over unused days within these statutory windows.

### United Kingdom
| Tenure | Annual PTO Days |
|--------|----------------|
| 0–2 years | 25 days |
| 2–5 years | 27 days |
| 5+ years | 30 days |

UK employees receive 8 statutory bank holidays in addition to PTO. PTO does not roll over; unused days are forfeited at the end of the holiday year (April 5).

### Germany
- All employees receive **28 days** per year.
- Rollover is permitted until March 31 of the following year if carry-over is requested and approved by a manager before December 15.
- Works council agreements may grant additional days at specific offices.

---

## 3. PTO Requests and Approval

- PTO requests must be submitted via Workday at least **5 business days** in advance for requests of 3 or more consecutive days.
- Requests of fewer than 3 days may be submitted with shorter notice, subject to manager approval.
- No employee may take more than **15 consecutive calendar days** of PTO without SVP-level approval.
- During company-wide blackout periods (typically the last 2 weeks of each fiscal quarter), PTO requests require VP-level approval.

---

## 4. PTO During Leave of Absence

Employees on approved unpaid leave do not accrue PTO. Employees on paid leave (e.g., paid parental leave) continue to accrue PTO at their standard rate.

---

## 5. PTO at Termination

- **Voluntary or involuntary termination (non-California US):** Accrued but unused PTO is **not** paid out unless required by state law. Check the State Supplements appendix for states with mandatory payout laws (e.g., Illinois, Colorado, Montana).
- **California:** Payout is mandatory (see Section 2, California).
- **Netherlands:** All accrued and unused PTO is paid out at termination.
- **UK and Germany:** Payout of accrued unused PTO is required by law.

---

## 6. Sick Time

Sick time is separate from PTO for all US employees effective January 1, 2024 (previously combined). Employees receive:
- **US:** 10 days sick time per year, non-accruing, non-rolling, non-payable at termination.
- **Netherlands/UK/Germany:** Sick leave is handled under statutory sick pay regulations and is separate from PTO entirely.

---

## 7. Exceptions

All exceptions to this policy require written approval from the VP of People Operations and must be documented in Workday.
